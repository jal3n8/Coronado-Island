Coronado Island · Est. 1886
An Island Unlike
Any Other
A Navy base, a legendary hotel, a town that somehow stayed itself. Here's what you're standing on.
1880s
The Vision
Elisha Babcock Jr. and Hampton Story purchased the entire Coronado peninsula for $110,000 in 1885 — a massive gamble. Their plan: a hotel so grand it would be "the talk of the western world."
1888
Hotel del Coronado Opens
Built in just 11 months with 2,000 workers, the Hotel del Coronado opened on February 19, 1888. It was the largest electrically lit building outside New York City. Thomas Edison personally oversaw the electrical installation.
1898
Oz Is Born Here
L. Frank Baum wrote "The Wonderful Wizard of Oz" while staying at the Hotel del Coronado. Local legend holds that the Emerald City's architecture was inspired by the hotel's distinctive Victorian towers.
1915
The Navy Arrives
Naval Air Station North Island was established, making Coronado the "birthplace of Naval Aviation." Charles Lindbergh departed from NAS North Island in 1927 on the Spirit of St. Louis's cross-country leg before his transatlantic flight.
1958
Some Like It Hot
Billy Wilder filmed the iconic Marilyn Monroe and Jack Lemmon comedy "Some Like It Hot" at the Hotel del Coronado. The hotel stood in for the fictional Seminole-Ritz Hotel in Florida. Monroe's pink bedroom suite is still a guest room today.
1969
The Bridge Changes Everything
The Coronado Bridge opened August 3, 1969, replacing the ferry system that had connected the island to San Diego since 1886. At 2.1 miles long and curving 80 degrees, it remains one of the most beautiful bridges in the country.
Today
A Town That Stayed Itself
Despite being minutes from a major city, Coronado maintained its small-town DNA. No chain restaurants on Orange Ave. No skyscrapers. The same families have run businesses here for generations. That's what you're feeling when it feels like home.
✦ Things Worth Knowing
⭐
The Glittering Sand
Coronado's sand contains small amounts of mica and garnet, giving it a dark, sparkling quality unlike any other California beach. It's been rated one of the top beaches in the US for decades.
🌊
SEAL Beach
The Naval Special Warfare Command (Navy SEALs) is headquartered here. BUD/S training — the most grueling military training in the world — happens on Coronado beach. Those early morning footprints are theirs.
🏨
14 U.S. Presidents
The Hotel del Coronado has hosted more US presidents than any other hotel in the world. From Benjamin Harrison in 1891 to Barack Obama in 2013. JFK met Marilyn Monroe here. Reagan had a cottage on the grounds.
👑
The Windsor Waltz
In 1920, the Prince of Wales met an American socialite named Wallis Spencer at a party in the Hotel del Coronado. She later became Wallis Simpson — the woman he abdicated the British throne for. It may have started here.

🌊 Coronado Beach Walk
Easy~3.6 mi~1.5 hrsOcean Views
Main coastal walk along Ocean Blvd. Start at Hotel Del and walk north — the crowd disappears fast and the dark sparkling sand stretches out wide.
💡 Golden hour heading north. Bring nothing. Stay as long as you want.
🏙 Ferry Landing to Tidelands
Easy~2.7 mi~55 minSkyline Views
From Centennial Park along San Diego Bay through Tidelands Park. SD skyline the entire way. Paved, flat, and genuinely stunning.
💡 Morning before the marine layer rolls in. Grab Trident Coffee first.
🌿 Orange Ave Wander
Easy~1 mi20–45 minTown + Shops
Hotel Del to Ferry Landing along Coronado's main street. Every café, restaurant, and local shop is here. Side streets have stunning 1880s Victorian homes.
💡 Peek down the side streets — one of the most intact Victorian neighborhoods in California.
🌅 Glorietta Bay Loop
Easy~1.5 mi~30 minBay + Marina
Loop around the bay park and marina. Calm, quiet, almost no tourists. The one locals actually walk every single day.
💡 You'll feel the pace difference immediately. Nods from neighbors. Boats in the water. Real.
🛣 Silver Strand Path
LongUp to 7 miBike recommendedOcean + Bay Both Sides
South from Ferry Landing — Pacific on your right, San Diego Bay on your left simultaneously. Paved, wild, beautiful. Rent bikes at Bikes & Beyond.
💡 Even 2–3 miles in is worth it. Novo Brewery at the midpoint.
